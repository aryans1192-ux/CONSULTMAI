# Framework: Compliance and Legal Risk
**Framework Name:** Risk Register + Remediation Roadmap
**Problem Type:** compliance or legal risk
**Tags:** legal, compliance, regulation, penalty, fine, audit, lawsuit, GDPR, liability, tax

## Hypothesis Tree — Root Cause Questions
- Is the exposure driven by a gap in current compliance processes, a change in regulation that has not yet been addressed, or a historical practice now under scrutiny?
- What is the maximum financial and operational penalty if the current exposure becomes a formal regulatory action?
- Do we have internal counsel with the relevant regulatory expertise, or do we need specialist external counsel immediately?
- Is this risk isolated to one jurisdiction, product, or business line — or does it require a company-wide remediation programme?

## Priority Flow
Assess and document exposure → Engage counsel → Build remediation roadmap → Embed compliance governance

## Execution Playbook

### PHASE 1 — IMMEDIATE (0–48 hrs): assessing and documenting the exposure
- Step 1: Document the full scope of the compliance or legal exposure in a confidential memo — General Counsel or CEO drafts this within 24 hours under legal privilege where possible; output is a written exposure summary stating: what is at risk, the relevant regulation or legal basis, the maximum financial penalty, and the estimated probability of enforcement action.
- Step 2: Engage specialist external counsel if the exposure involves a regulatory body, litigation threat, or jurisdiction where internal expertise is insufficient — CEO authorises this engagement within 24 hours; output is a confirmed external counsel engagement with a scope of work and a 48-hour briefing deadline.
- Step 3: Implement an immediate operational hold on any activity that is directly creating or worsening the exposure — COO and General Counsel co-own this hold decision within 48 hours; output is a written directive stopping the specific activity and a communication to relevant teams explaining the hold without unnecessary disclosure.

### PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSIS (Days 3–7): full exposure mapping and remediation design
- Step 4: Conduct a full internal audit of all processes, systems, and historical practices related to the compliance gap — General Counsel and Compliance lead own this; output is a comprehensive gap analysis document listing every instance of non-compliance, the period it occurred, and the affected parties.
- Step 5: Obtain a written legal opinion on the maximum exposure, the enforcement likelihood, and the mitigating actions that reduce liability — external counsel delivers this; output by Day 5 is a privileged legal opinion memo with a recommended response strategy.
- Step 6: Determine whether voluntary disclosure to the regulator is advisable — this often reduces penalties materially — General Counsel and CEO decide this in consultation with external counsel; output by Day 7 is a decision memo on disclosure approach with the rationale and timing.

### PHASE 3 — STABILISATION (Weeks 2–4): executing the remediation programme
- Step 7: Build a formal remediation plan that closes every identified compliance gap — Compliance lead and General Counsel co-own this; output is a written remediation roadmap with each gap, the fix required, the owner, and the completion deadline.
- Step 8: Implement the highest-priority remediation actions — those that directly stop the exposure from growing — relevant operational leads execute under Compliance oversight; output is documented evidence that the exposure-generating activities have been stopped or corrected.
- Step 9: Prepare a regulatory response strategy — whether that is voluntary disclosure, cooperation with an existing inquiry, or a proactive engagement — General Counsel leads this with external counsel; output is an approved response strategy and a communication plan.

### PHASE 4 — RECOVERY (Month 2–3): embedding compliance governance
- Step 10: Implement a compliance monitoring programme covering all regulatory obligations relevant to the business — Compliance lead owns this build; output is a live compliance dashboard tracking key regulatory metrics with alert thresholds.
- Step 11: Conduct company-wide compliance training on the specific regulatory area where the gap was identified — General Counsel or Compliance lead designs this; output is 100% completion rate for all relevant staff with a signed acknowledgement log.
- Step 12: Deliver a board-ready Compliance Recovery Report at Month 3 — General Counsel and CEO present together; output documents the exposure identified, remediation actions completed, interim controls deployed, evidence of no further violations, and the compliance governance system now embedded.
