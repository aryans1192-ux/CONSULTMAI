# Framework: Competitive Pressure
**Framework Name:** Competitive Moat Analysis
**Problem Type:** competitive pressure
**Tags:** competitor, market share, losing deals, price war, undercutting, disruption, rival

## Hypothesis Tree — Root Cause Questions
- Is the competitor winning on price alone, or have they made a genuine product, distribution, or brand move that changes the value equation?
- Which customer segments are most at risk of switching, and which are the most loyal — and why?
- Do we have a genuinely defensible moat (switching costs, network effect, proprietary data, brand) or are we competing on features that can be copied?
- Is the competitive threat from an existing player escalating, or a new entrant disrupting the category from below?

## Priority Flow
Map the competitive attack → Find defensible edge → Choose and commit to response → Arm the team

## Execution Playbook

### PHASE 1 — IMMEDIATE (0–48 hrs): understanding the competitive attack
- Step 1: Document every known competitor move in the last 90 days — pricing changes, product launches, partnerships, sales hires, and marketing campaigns — Strategy or a designated competitive intelligence owner completes this within 24 hours; output is a structured competitive update memo shared with all leadership.
- Step 2: Pull your last 60 days of lost deals data and tag every loss by stated reason — Sales Director owns this; output is a ranked loss analysis showing what percentage of losses cite price, product gaps, relationship, or brand as the primary reason.
- Step 3: Brief the Sales team with factual competitive talking points so they stop losing deals to misinformation — Sales Director and CMO co-own this brief within 48 hours; output is a one-page competitive battle card for each major competitor, distributed to all quota-carrying reps.

### PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSIS (Days 3–7): identifying your defensible edge
- Step 4: Map your customer base by segment and score each segment on: switching cost, relationship depth, and dependency on your specific capability — Strategy lead owns this; output is a customer segment map showing which segments are truly defensible and which are at active risk.
- Step 5: Conduct a feature-by-feature and value-driver comparison against your top 3 competitors — Product and Strategy leads co-own this; output is a competitive matrix that honestly names where you are ahead, at parity, and behind.
- Step 6: Identify your single most defensible moat — the one capability, relationship, or asset that is genuinely hard for a competitor to replicate in 12 months — CEO and Strategy lead determine this; output by Day 7 is a written moat definition and the strategic response options that double down on it.

### PHASE 3 — STABILISATION (Weeks 2–4): executing the competitive response
- Step 7: Make an explicit strategic choice: price to win, differentiate on a specific dimension, or cede the contested segments and double down on defensible ones — CEO makes this call; output is a written strategic direction memo that stops the organisation from trying all three at once.
- Step 8: Restructure sales resources to concentrate coverage on your most defensible customer segments and pull back from segments where you are structurally disadvantaged — Sales Director owns this reallocation; output is a revised territory and account priority model.
- Step 9: Launch 2–3 product or service improvements specifically designed to widen the moat in your most defensible segment — Product and Engineering leads own this; output is a prioritised feature roadmap with the competitive rationale for each item clearly stated.

### PHASE 4 — RECOVERY (Month 2–3): building competitive intelligence as a capability
- Step 10: Implement a monthly competitive intelligence review covering pricing moves, product launches, customer wins/losses, and hiring signals — Strategy lead chairs this; output is a standing monthly review with a live competitive tracker.
- Step 11: Build a win/loss analysis programme so that every significant deal outcome is coded and reviewed — Sales Ops owns this; output is a quarterly win/loss report with trend data showing whether competitive position is improving or deteriorating.
- Step 12: Deliver a board-ready Competitive Position Report at Month 3 — CEO presents; output documents the competitive threat identified, strategic response chosen, early indicators of position recovery, and the competitive intelligence system now in place.
