# Framework: Leadership Misalignment
**Framework Name:** Decision Rights + Priority Cascade
**Problem Type:** leadership misalignment
**Tags:** leadership conflict, strategy unclear, vision, executives disagree, no alignment, management conflict

## Hypothesis Tree — Root Cause Questions
- Is the disagreement about strategic direction (where to compete), resource allocation (how to prioritise), or execution accountability (who owns what)?
- Is this a new conflict triggered by a specific event — funding round, key hire, board pressure — or a long-standing cultural pattern?
- Does the organisation have a clear decision-making framework, or do major decisions require unanimous consensus that never comes?
- Is the misalignment contained to the executive team, or has it already cascaded into team-level confusion and conflicting priorities?

## Priority Flow
Surface disagreements explicitly → Force priority alignment → Assign decision rights → Cascade and govern

## Execution Playbook

### PHASE 1 — IMMEDIATE (0–48 hrs): making the misalignment visible and bounded
- Step 1: Convene an off-site or structured session with the full leadership team to explicitly name the top 3 strategic disagreements — CEO facilitates or brings in an external facilitator within 48 hours; output is a written list of the specific disagreements, not the symptoms, shared with the full leadership team.
- Step 2: Identify which decisions are currently blocked or inconsistent because of the misalignment — COO and relevant leads audit this within 24 hours; output is a list of the top 5 decisions that need to be made or reversed to unblock execution.
- Step 3: Establish a temporary decision-making protocol — until full alignment is reached, which single person has the final call on which categories of decision — CEO establishes this within 48 hours; output is a written escalation protocol shared with the leadership team.

### PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSIS (Days 3–7): diagnosing the root cause of the misalignment
- Step 4: Conduct individual 60-minute structured interviews with each leadership team member to understand their position on the key disagreements — CEO or board chair conducts these; output is a synthesis document showing where genuine strategic differences exist vs. where the disagreement is personal or process-based.
- Step 5: Assess whether the board or key shareholders are contributing to the misalignment by sending conflicting signals — CEO and Chair address this directly; output is a written alignment on the board's actual mandate and what decisions are within management's authority.
- Step 6: Determine whether the leadership team composition is the right one for the next stage of the company — CEO assesses this honestly; output by Day 7 is a written assessment of whether every executive is the right person for the phase ahead, with any personnel decisions acknowledged but not yet acted on.

### PHASE 3 — STABILISATION (Weeks 2–4): establishing alignment and decision rights
- Step 7: Run a structured strategic alignment session using a framework like OKRs or a one-page strategy map to force explicit priority choices — CEO facilitates with the full leadership team; output is a written one-page strategy document with the top 3 company priorities for the next 6 months, signed off by all leaders.
- Step 8: Define and publish decision rights using a RACI or equivalent framework for the top 20 recurring decisions in the company — COO owns this documentation; output is a decision rights document agreed by the leadership team and published to all managers.
- Step 9: Address any personnel issues identified in Phase 2 — role changes, scope adjustments, or exits — CEO acts on these; output is a confirmed leadership structure that is stable and unambiguous for the next 12 months.

### PHASE 4 — RECOVERY (Month 2–3): institutionalising alignment
- Step 10: Implement a monthly leadership team review with a fixed agenda: strategy progress, top 3 cross-functional blockers, and a specific decision that needs to be made — CEO chairs this; output is a standing meeting cadence with pre-read materials and a decision log.
- Step 11: Cascade the strategic priorities to every team in the form of quarterly OKRs or equivalent — each executive is accountable for their team's alignment; output is a full-company OKR set with clear line-of-sight from company priorities to team goals.
- Step 12: Deliver a board-ready Leadership Alignment Report at Month 3 — CEO presents; output documents the misalignment identified, decisions made to resolve it, the strategic priorities now in place, and the governance cadence that will maintain alignment.
