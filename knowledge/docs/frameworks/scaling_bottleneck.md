# Framework: Scaling Bottleneck
**Framework Name:** Constraint Identification + 2x Build
**Problem Type:** scaling bottleneck
**Tags:** scale, capacity, infrastructure, overwhelmed, rapid growth, volume, growing fast

## Hypothesis Tree — Root Cause Questions
- What is the single binding constraint — people, technology, process, or capital — and at what volume does it break?
- Is the constraint in the core delivery mechanism, or in support functions (onboarding, billing, compliance) that slow down the customer journey?
- Are we trying to scale a process that is fundamentally not scalable without redesign, or just under-resourced?
- Do we have sufficient lead time to relieve the constraint before current demand growth hits the ceiling?

## Priority Flow
Identify binding constraint → Quantify the gap → Remove the constraint → Build to 2x capacity

## Execution Playbook

### PHASE 1 — IMMEDIATE (0–48 hrs): identifying and quantifying the constraint
- Step 1: Map the end-to-end delivery process and mark every stage with its current throughput capacity vs. current demand — COO or CTO owns this within 24 hours; output is a capacity map showing each stage's maximum throughput and the current utilisation rate.
- Step 2: Identify the single stage where demand most consistently exceeds capacity — this is the binding constraint — COO confirms this within 24 hours; output is a written constraint statement: the bottleneck stage, its current capacity, and what breaks first if volume grows another 20%.
- Step 3: Calculate the lead time to relieve the constraint — how many weeks to hire, build, or procure enough to double the constrained stage's capacity — Operations and HR leads own this estimate; output is a timeline with critical path milestones clearly stated.

### PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSIS (Days 3–7): designing the constraint removal
- Step 4: Assess whether the constraint is a temporary under-investment (add resources to the existing process) or a structural design flaw (the process must be redesigned before it can scale) — COO leads this assessment with input from the team closest to the constraint; output is a written determination with the recommended approach.
- Step 5: Identify quick-win capacity improvements at the constraint stage that can be implemented within 2 weeks without a full redesign — Ops and Engineering leads jointly own this; output is a list of 3–5 immediate actions that can buy capacity while the structural fix is built.
- Step 6: Design the full constraint removal plan — what needs to be built, hired, bought, or redesigned, in what sequence — COO owns this; output by Day 7 is a constraint removal roadmap with owners, timelines, cost estimates, and the capacity target.

### PHASE 3 — STABILISATION (Weeks 2–4): executing quick wins and starting the structural build
- Step 7: Implement the quick-win capacity improvements identified in Phase 2 — Ops lead owns this; output is measurable capacity increase at the constraint stage by end of Week 2, with before/after throughput documented.
- Step 8: Begin the structural constraint removal — hiring, system builds, or process redesign as specified in the roadmap — relevant leads own their workstreams; output is active progress on the structural fix with a clear completion date.
- Step 9: Implement a demand management or queue management system at the constraint to protect quality during the transition — COO owns this; output is a visible queue management process that prevents quality degradation while capacity is being built.

### PHASE 4 — RECOVERY (Month 2–3): building to 2x capacity with headroom
- Step 10: Complete the structural constraint removal and validate that the constrained stage can handle 2x current demand — Operations lead certifies this; output is a documented capacity test showing throughput at 2x current volume without quality or SLA degradation.
- Step 11: Implement a forward capacity planning process that triggers a build cycle when demand is forecast to hit 70% of current capacity — COO owns this; output is a standing capacity review cadence and a demand forecast integrated with the operational planning cycle.
- Step 12: Deliver a board-ready Scaling Readiness Report at Month 3 — COO presents; output documents the constraint identified, capacity built, current headroom, and the proactive capacity planning system now in place.
